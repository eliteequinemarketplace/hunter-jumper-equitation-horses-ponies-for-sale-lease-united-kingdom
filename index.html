The United Kingdom has a rich and distinctive equestrian tradition — home to the British Show Jumping Association (BSJA), the Horse of the Year Show, the Royal International Horse Show, and a strong working hunter and show horse culture that is unique in the equestrian world. Elite Equine Marketplace connects US and international buyers with UK-based sellers, offering access to quality British and Irish sport horses that are often overlooked by buyers focused exclusively on Dutch and Belgian warmbloods.

BSJA Show Jumping — Britain's Show Jumping Circuit

The British Show Jumping Association governs show jumping competition across the UK — from grassroots Newcomers classes through Premier League and Nations Cup competition. BSJA-affiliated horses competing at the higher levels have documented records in a respected national competition system, and British show jumping consistently produces horses of international quality. Many successful FEI horses have BSJA backgrounds.

The Working Hunter Tradition

The British working hunter is a uniquely British horse type — judged on a combination of jumping ability, movement, conformation, and presentation under saddle. Working hunters are naturally athletic, well-built horses with an exceptional all-round way of going that translates well to the American hunter ring. UK working hunters with top show records represent outstanding potential imports for US hunter buyers seeking natural movement and a genuine hunter type.

Horse of the Year Show (HOYS)

The Horse of the Year Show, held annually at the NEC in Birmingham, is the UK's most prestigious qualification-only indoor equestrian event — the pinnacle of British show horse competition across every discipline. HOYS-qualified horses represent the very best of British show horses and carry the most prestigious credential available in the UK market.
